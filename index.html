Introduction

Arknights is a mobile science-fantasy tower defense gacha video game developed by Hypergryph for Android and IOS.

Arknights is currently avaliable in five regions/servers:

  1. Mainland China (CN)
  2. Japan (JP)
  3. Korea (KR)
  4. Worldwide (EN)
  5. Taiwan/Hong kong/Macau(TW)

The JP, KR and EN are released and updated simultaneously; for this reason the community collectively refers to them as the Global/EN server

Synopsis

Arknights takes place in the dystopian world of Terra. Terra may seem initially similar to our Earth, a modern world with a relatively similar level of technology, perhaps slightly more futuristic. However, its inhabitants, the "Ancients", sport animalistic traits. The world is constantly devastated by Catastrophes, ecological disasters of various sorts, forcing the world's inhabitants to adopt a nomadic life through nomadic cities. Along with the Catastrophes came Originium, a black crystalline mineral left behind in the aftermath of catastrophes. Originium is widely used by the Terrans as a source of energy, engineering materials, and catalyzes magic-like powers known as Originium Arts. However, exposure to Originium leads to a "terminal disease" known as Oripathy, which has become a pandemic plaguing all of Terra. Oripathy is not easily transmissible, at least until the death and crystallization of its victims. Nevertheless, those stricken with Oripathy are known as Infected and suffer heavy oppression and marginalization across the entire world.

Gameplay

Those who have played tower defense(TD) games will be familiar with Arknights' gameplay: the player must keep enemies from reaching a certain goal by using "towers".

However, unlike most TD games, Arknights is different by adding RPG elements and assigning mechanics and skills to each tower. A tower is called an Operator and can be deployed either to attack or block enemies. To deploy an Operator one must have the same number of Deployment Points(DP) required by each Operator. Their are two ways to accumulate DP, one is passive accumulation per second and the other is through the skills of some Operators. Enemies take various uncommon paths to reach their goals with a wide variety of enemy types and behaviors. Each map may introduce hazards to the operators for a more challenging and strategic gameplay.

Operators

Operators are the characters used in battle by the players. They serve as "towers" the player places in locations around the battlefield to prevent the enemies from reaching the objective.

Each Operator in Arknights belong to one of eight classes which determines how they function, with each class having their subclasses that specializes the Operator's abilities

Classes

Vanguards

They're the cheapest, and they get access to DP recovery skills. Their stats are lower than other melee operators but they help you deploy the rest of your team faster. Best in fending of weaker enemies at the start and will be replaced with an operator best suited for the job.

Vanguard subclass
  • Charger-generates 1 DP per kill and refunds all of the DP cost when retreated
  • Pioneer-steady generate DP with skills
  • Standard Bearer- Rapid generate DP with skills but can't attack and block when skill is active
  • Tactician- Ranged, can deploy reinforcement within range

Guards

The DPS units with high physical atk but low DEF. They're perfect for dealing with strong enemies.

Guard subclasses
  • Dreadnought- Focuses on dueling enemies one-on-one
  • Tresher- Attacks hit multiple targets equal to block count
  • Noble- Ranged attack that deals 80% damage
  • Bladeweaver- Attack deals Arts Damage
  • Brawler- Fast attack
  • Fencer- attack deliver two hits
  • Martial- Cannot be directly healed by friendlies; restore HP in each attack
  • Instructor- Supports allies with buffs

Defenders

The tank units with high DEF and low ATK with a block count of 3 units. Perfect for giving your DPS units more time to kill a bunch of strong enemies.

Defender subclasses
  • Protector-Focuses on tanking enemy attacks
  • Preserver-can heal friendly units with skills
  • Resolute-cannot be directly healed by allieas but can heal themselves through skills or talents
  • Arts Protector-Attack deals Arts damage while the skill is active
  • Champion-Can only block one enemy
  • Fortress-Uses a short-ranged attack that deals AOE damage when not blocking enemies

Snipers

The ranged physical DPS units. They're very good at dealing with light units, but their damage is close to 0 when it comes to heavily armored enemies.

Sniper subclasses
  • Marksman-Attakcs one target at a time and prioritizes targeting aerial anemies
  • Bombardier- Attacks deal AOE damage
  • Pinpoint-Has a short attack range but high ATK
  • Deadeye-Has a long attack range and prioritizes enemeies with the lowest DEF
  • Siege-Has a minimum range of the frontal tiles and prioritizes enemies with the highes weight first.
  • Annihilator-Attacks deal two consecutive hits; can only attack ground enemies
  • Spreadshooter-Attacks damage all enemies in range, with the enemey right ahead taking more damage.

Casters

Casters have shorter range, slower ATSPD and higher DP cost compared to Snipers, but they deal Arts damage instead suited for dealing with heavy armored enemies.

Caster subclasses
  • Core- Attacks one target at a time
  • Splash- Attacks deal AOE damage
  • Mystic- Store attacks when not attacking and simultaneously releases all stored attacks
  • Drone- Controls a drone that attacks independently dealing more damage in successive hits
  • Chain- Attacks jump between multiple targets and slows them, but deals less damage each jump
  • Blast-Attacks damage all enemies in a long linear range
  • Guardian-Only attack when the skill is active, which damage all enemies in range

Medics

They're ranged units that can heal allies within their range. The amount of healing is equivalent to their ATK stat.

Medic subclasses
  • Healer - heals one target at a time
  • Ringhealer - Heals up to three targets simultaneously
  • Mender - Heals targets at a wide range with having less effective healing on further targets

Supporters

Is a self-explanatory class. Some buff teammates, some debuff enemies. They can also directly attack the enemy dealing magic damage and slowing the enemy down.

Supporter subclasses
  • Stopper- Attacks briefly slow enemies
  • Muse - Can't attack but continuously heal allies within range; is capable of buffing allies through skills
  • Bless- Augments the defense and survivability of allies
  • Curse- Provides debuff to the enemies
  • Summoner- Can utilize summoned units

Specialists

This class has operators in the most niche roles and would depend on the players knowledge to know which map to use the operators in this category. Operators on this subclass have diverse skillsets.

Specialist subclasses
  • Push- can be deployed on ranged tiles; can push away enemies with skills
  • Pull- can be deployed on ranged tiles; can pull enemies with skills
  • Executioner- has much shorter redeployment
  • Peddler- has short redeployment CD but drains 3 DP every 3 seconds while deployed and will automatically retreate if there are not enough DP
  • Stalker- Hits all surrounding enemies and less likely to be targeted by enemies
  • Trapper- ranged units that can use traps to deal damage
  • Oddball- ranged units that causes friendly fire to buff allies

Tips

Variety is encouraged. Relying on a single Operator class and/or archetype is not enough to clear most operations. Make a diverse team to handle any and every possibility instead.

Rarity does not always make a difference. While it is true that higher-rarity Operators tend to be superior to lower-rarity ones, the latter can still be viable in general situations and useful as reserves in certain situations due to their low DP cost and the fact that they are cheaper to build.

DP cost efficiency matters. Utilizing powerful Operators, usually, those of higher rarity may be tempting, but note that most of them have high DP costs. Add several 3★ and/or 4★ to the mix. Some stages have DP economy as a challenge of its own. Make sure operators are worth what they cost to you and consider that sometimes you could solve the situation with someone much cheaper.

Don't be afraid to fail, and use your drill plans. Theorizing is all well and good, but practice makes perfect. It is difficult, or even sometimes impossible to know what to expect out of a stage. Failure is not truly a failure, but a learning experience allowing you to remediate the flaws of your strategy. Different players learn at different speeds, and it is truly possible to improve as a player by simply trying.

Use the map previews! Looking at a map's layout is a great, if not necessary step to see which team you can attempt to bring, how many chokepoints you will have to hold, and what might be in store for you. Pay attention, for example, to the presence of Aerial Incursion Points that signify you will need an operator that can hit drones.

Remember to have fun. If you find yourself bored by succeeding at stages using cookie-cutter strategies, try to make your own fun and spice up the game by challenging yourself to certain self-imposed rules, or by trying out your more underappreciated operators. Remember, variety is encouraged, and what low-rarity operators lack in firepower they tend to make up for in specialization. Arknights is a game, it should not be a chore.
